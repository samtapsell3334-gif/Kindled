**Runtime:** 90s · **Placement:** behind the investor PIN gate only ·
**Figures:** all match the investor page (£3.2bn OnePoll/Halifax; £250k ask; 0.5% take;
two-phase revenue). **No buzzwords. British English. Captions-first.**

Format: `[start–end] SCENE | ON-SCREEN TEXT | VO line`

---

[00–08] **Cold open — the Ignition signature.** Match-spark, embers rise, warm bloom.
ON-SCREEN: "Britain wastes £3.2 billion a year on unwanted gifts." *(Source: OnePoll/Halifax, 2023)*
VO: "Britain wastes three point two billion pounds a year on gifts nobody wanted."

[08–20] **The product in one breath.** A pot fills as contributor chips land; a sealed
flame; a face lighting up (illustrated in brand style, not stock).
ON-SCREEN: "Create a pot → share one link → everyone chips in → sealed until the big day → the reveal."
VO: "Kindled is group gifting done properly. Create a pot, share one link, everyone chips in — and it stays sealed until the big day, when the reveal brings it all together."

[20–40] **Money on every outcome.** Split screen. LEFT: full pot → gift-card conversion → commission ticker. RIGHT: partial pot → equal celebration → gift card (commission) OR stacked forward (retention).
ON-SCREEN: "Every pot ends in revenue or retention. Usually both."
VO: "A fully funded pot converts to retailer gift cards — the retailer pays us a commission. A partly funded pot is revealed with just as much joy — taken as a gift card, or stacked forward to the next occasion. Every pot ends in revenue or retention. Usually both."

[40–55] **The Trojan horse.** A child's list radiates to grandparents, aunts, friends; each chip-in lights a new user node.
ON-SCREEN: "Kids' lists first. Every contributor becomes an activated user."
VO: "We start with kids' lists — the most habitual gifting occasion there is. Parents create the list; grandparents, aunts and friends chip in with zero set-up — and every one of them finishes as an activated user with a payment method. The child's pot is the acquisition channel."

[55–65] **The flywheel.** The loop animates: pot → contributors → their pots → their contributors.
ON-SCREEN: "Pot → contributors → their pots → repeat. That's the K-factor."
VO: "Contributors are nudged into pots of their own — a log burner, or a Maldives fund — and the loop turns again, bigger each time."

[65–78] **Roadmap.** Two-phase timeline; revenue lines appear per phase; clean-room path as a third, clearly-labelled step.
ON-SCREEN: "Phase 1: gift card commissions · Phase 2: retail media on first-party gifting intent · Then: clean-room partnerships (roadmap)."
VO: "Phase one earns from gift card commissions, live from launch. Phase two is retail media, built on first-party gifting intent that we own. The clean-room path comes after — stated honestly, that's roadmap."
